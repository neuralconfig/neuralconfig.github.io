My journey from network engineer to AI developer

My Story

For over 20 years, I've built and operated enterprise network infrastructure—from routing protocols and wireless architectures to zero-trust security. But I always felt there was more we could do with automation.

Then AI happened. Not the hype, but the real capabilities of LLMs and agentic systems. I saw an opportunity to bridge two worlds: the deep infrastructure knowledge most AI developers don't have, and the modern AI capabilities most network engineers haven't explored.

01

The Foundation: Network Infrastructure

Started building enterprise networks in healthcare and education. Learned that production systems need more than just technical specs—they need reliability, security, and practical operation.

02

The Pivot: Discovering AI

Completed Stanford's Machine Learning specialization. Built first AI agent for network automation. Realized the potential of combining infrastructure expertise with AI capabilities.

03

Building in the Open

Started sharing tools on GitHub. Autonomous agents, RAG systems, zero-touch provisioning—all open source. The community response showed others need this intersection too.

04

What's Next

Expanding into iOS development. Writing about the technical challenges. Looking to work with AI companies or network vendors who value both skill sets.
